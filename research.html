Research

From geometric representations to deployable medical and visual intelligence

My work is organized around three connected research lines: 3D geometric processing and reconstruction, intelligent graphics/image processing, and multimodal dental/medical data processing. These lines share a common goal: integrating geometric priors, learning-based representations, and optimization to solve difficult visual computing problems.

Research Methodology

Core technical themes

Geometry-driven learning

Embedding cross fields, implicit functions, landmarks, contours, motion constraints, and structural priors into deep models to improve stability, interpretability, and data efficiency.

Multimodal representation

Fusing point clouds, RGB/depth images, oral scans, CBCT, and facial images for tasks where a single modality is incomplete, noisy, or ambiguous.

Application-oriented optimization

Designing algorithms that respect practical constraints in fabrication, orthodontic treatment planning, medical data quality, and clinical deployment scenarios.

Collaboration Topics

Potential collaborative directions

I am interested in collaborations on geometry processing, dental/medical AI, 3D vision and generation, multimodal data analysis, and applied visual computing systems.
